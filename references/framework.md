# The AI Competency Framework

Published by AI Enablement Academy (aienablement.academy). Licensed CC BY 4.0. The cell wording below is verbatim from the published framework — do not paraphrase it when presenting the framework itself.

A dual-axis framework:

- **Enablement Layer** (across) — the impact radius of what you do with AI.
- **Capability Level** (down) — how deep your expertise goes.

## The three Enablement Layers

| Layer | Subtitle | Impact radius |
|---|---|---|
| **Augmentation** | Personal Productivity | You, personally, getting faster. Custom GPTs, Gems, Claude skills, your own chatbots. It only touches your output. |
| **Automation** | Process Layer | The radius widens: your team's processes shift, with ripple effects on the people around you. |
| **Agentic** | System Innovation | The widest radius: designing whole systems, not just workflows. |

These are three different things. Much of the industry blurs them into one word ("agent"), which is exactly what makes AI capability hard to name and teach. Keep them distinct.

## The four Capability Levels

Foundations → Essentials → Fluency → Native. How far your skill goes *within* each layer.

## The twelve cells (verbatim)

### Augmentation — Personal Productivity

- **Foundations:** Identify appropriate AI use cases in daily work and select AI tools that match task requirements.
- **Essentials:** Produce AI assets and artifacts using structured prompting with context control.
- **Fluency:** Design and create custom AI assistants and tools integrated into your stack.
- **Native:** Operate with an AI-first personal workflow where automation and agentic delegation are default modes.

### Automation — Process Layer

- **Foundations:** Map existing processes to identify automation opportunities with conditional logic and error handling.
- **Essentials:** Build AI-enabled interactive tools using no-code platforms. Take vibecoding seriously.
- **Fluency:** Create multi-step workflows with agentic nodes and platform-integrated integrations.
- **Native:** Deploy production-grade automation infrastructure with versioning and handoff protocols.

### Agentic — System Innovation

- **Foundations:** Apply agentic system design principles to architect AI-native solutions with context management and memory.
- **Essentials:** Design organizational knowledge systems with MCP integrations, custom skills, and documented decision logic.
- **Fluency:** Build production-ready agentic systems with PRD-driven development, testing protocols, and deployment.
- **Native:** Scale agentic systems across teams with governance frameworks and organizational memory architecture.

## How to read it

Not as a ladder you climb, but as a map. A person places themselves in each column separately — deep in one layer and early in another is normal. Where the axes meet is a real, nameable capability: "I can build a no-code tool" sits in a different box than "I can design an agentic system my whole team relies on." Both matter; neither is the full picture.

## Known limitation (named by the authors)

The grid's clean layout suggests each step takes equal effort. It doesn't. Jumping from Foundations to Essentials in Augmentation is a different kind of leap than jumping from Fluency to Native in Agentic. Treat step sizes as unequal, and say so when coaching someone facing one of the big leaps.
