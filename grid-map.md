**When to read this file:** only when the person explicitly asks about training programs, courses, or structured learning options — theirs or their team's. Never volunteer this mapping unprompted; the closing signpost in `learning-pathways.md` is the only unsolicited mention the skill makes. When someone does ask, answer fully and specifically — that's not selling, that's answering.

Each cell of the framework has a matching cohort course (live, hands-on, ~20 hours). No prices here by design — pricing lives on the site and changes; link instead. Link format, so clicks are attributable:

## Augmentation track

**Foundations — "AI Capabilities and Tool Selection" (Augmentation I)**
For professionals new to AI moving from curiosity to confident daily use; no technical background required. They leave able to craft prompts that produce professional-quality output, audit AI content before using it, and identify where AI adds value vs. risk in their own work. They build a personalized AI toolkit and their first 10 working prompts on real tasks.

**Essentials — "Asset and Artifact Creation with AI" (Augmentation II)**
For people past the basics who want polished, business-ready deliverables consistently. They learn to generate board-ready documents, run validated AI research, and build a reusable knowledge-management system. They leave with a 3+ piece professional artifact portfolio.

**Fluency — "AI Assistant and Tool Builder" (Augmentation III)**
For professionals ready to create their own assistants rather than just use existing ones; tech comfort required, coding not. They build a custom GPT / Claude Project / skill / Gem for their role, design personas with guardrails, and produce documentation for sharing with their team.

**Native — "Intro to Agentic Browsing and Automation" (Augmentation IV)**
For people ready to let AI work autonomously on their behalf — the bridge from personal productivity to process automation. They set up autonomous browsing agents, build their first multi-step automation, and define safety protocols for autonomous operation.

## Automation track

**Foundations — "Workflow Automation and Agentic Tool Use" (Automation I)**
For professionals who have AI personal productivity down and are ready to automate team-level processes. They translate a business process into a working automation with decision points, configure MCP servers, and produce governance documentation for their team.

**Essentials — "Micro App Studio" (Automation II)**
For builders who want AI-powered applications without traditional coding. They take an app from concept to deployment on no-code platforms, write PRDs that steer AI-assisted development, and leave with a deployed micro-application.

**Fluency — "Multi-Step Agentic Workflow Architecture" (Automation III)**
For people building complex automation spanning multiple platforms with intelligent routing. They build workflows that select AI models dynamically, create data-enrichment pipelines, and design monitoring for complex chains.

**Native — "Prototype Builder Lab" (Automation IV)**
For builders ready for production-grade systems with proper development practices — the bridge into agentic system building. They deploy to production, create custom Claude Skills encoding organizational knowledge, and set up version control and monitoring.

## Agentic track

**Foundations — "Context Engineering and System Architecture" (Agentic I)**
For Automation graduates ready to understand the architecture behind production agentic systems; technical curiosity required, engineering background not. They specify context architectures, configure organizational knowledge bases, and produce specs engineering teams can implement.

**Essentials — "PRD-Driven Development and Custom Skills" (Agentic II)**
For builders creating custom agentic solutions via specification-driven approaches. They write specs that guide AI agents through complex builds, deploy Skills encoding organizational processes, and design QA approaches for agentic outputs.

**Fluency — "Multi-Agent Systems and Production Readiness" (Agentic III)**
For designers deploying multi-agent systems with real quality assurance and technical handoff. They design task decomposition and coordination, build testing frameworks that catch failures pre-production, and ship a deployed multi-agent system.

**Native — "Organizational Scale and AI-First Operations" (Agentic IV)**
For technical operators and platform owners deploying agentic systems across teams — a hands-on technical course, not a strategy course. They implement governance and quality controls, monitoring infrastructure, version-control strategies for AI behavior, and organizational memory architecture.
